Bacterial Vaginosis (BV)


Description

Bacterial vaginosis is very common, most people who have a vagina get it at some point in their life and many get it several times. You usually get it without having had sex. The bacterial flora in the vagina changes when you have your period, if you have sex or for other reasons.


Symptoms

Bacterial vaginosis involves foul-smelling discharge from the vagina due to altered bacterial flora. The discharge is often runny and smells bad. It can sting and itch, but is often harmless.


Risk of Transmission

Studies are showing that bacterial vaginosis can be sexually transmitted between vaginas. If you have bacterial vaginosis and have a regular partner who also has a vagina, it may therefore be a good idea for your partner to also go to the gynecologist and get checked so that you can receive treatment at the same time.


Testing

Contact your health clinic for testing. The testing is done by looking at the fluor albus colour and shape, the phH value, if the smell gets fishy by adding KOH (Potassium hydroxide) and if wet smear exist in the clue cells.


Timeframe Before Testing

When you have symptoms.


Treatment

Bacterial vaginosis is treated with antibiotics by vaginal gel or vaginal tablets for 3-6 days depending on the medicine.


Good to Know

You might need to be treated several times because Bacterial Vaginosis can take time before it is gone permanently. Spirals might increase the risk of it returning.

Vaccines

There is currently no vaccine to prevent Bacterial Vaginosis.
